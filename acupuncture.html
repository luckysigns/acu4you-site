How It Works

About acupuncture

A plain-language introduction to qi, the meridians, and what actually happens during a treatment.

The flow of qi

Acupuncture works with qi (pronounced “chee”) — life energy, the electricity of the body. Qi travels from fingertips to toes, from the bones out to the skin, and along the way it can become blocked, stagnant, excessive, or depleted.

Very thin needles, placed just under the skin, stimulate qi and help restore its natural flow — bringing balance back to body, mind, emotions, and spirit.

Each of the five elements corresponds to an organ system, and each organ has its own meridian — an energy channel through the body. Through careful diagnosis and pulse-taking, Hilarey listens to how each system is working and reads the quality of qi throughout the body. From there, a treatment of specific points is chosen to help the body re-balance itself.

About those needles

Most people feel a brief sensation as the qi responds — a dull ache, a swirl of energy, gentle warmth, or a little zing. It typically fades moments after the needle is placed, and what follows most often is deep relaxation.

  • High-quality stainless steel, about the width of a human hair
  • Sterile, sealed, and used only once before being discarded
  • Placed just under the skin — this is not an injection

It supports physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual wellbeing, and it also shines as preventive care for maintaining a balanced, healthy life.
